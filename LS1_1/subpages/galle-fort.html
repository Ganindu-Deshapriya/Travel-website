Galle Fort

Standing strong against the waves for centuries, Galle Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a captivating example of fortified colonial architecture in Sri Lanka. Explore its ramparts, soak in the ocean views, and wander through its charming streets lined with boutiques, cafes, and historical buildings.

Originally built by the Portuguese in the 16th century, the fort was subsequently fortified by the Dutch. Today, it's a vibrant and walkable town, offering a glimpse into Sri Lanka's rich past. Immerse yourself in the unique atmosphere and discover the cultural heritage of this iconic landmark.
